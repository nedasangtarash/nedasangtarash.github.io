Animator and experimental artist exploring memory, the body, and childhood misunderstandings through moving images and material-based animation.

Neda Sangtarash (b. 2002, Isfahan) is an animator and experimental artist whose work explores memory, the body, and childhood misunderstandings through moving images.

She began her artistic practice with storyboard, background and character design, as well as pixel art, and later studied Handicrafts, developing an interdisciplinary approach grounded in experimentation with materials, form, and non-linear narration.

In her practice, animation becomes a space where image replaces language and movement allows access to suppressed or forgotten layers of experience.
