Neda Sangtarash

Animator & Experimental Artist

Exploring memory, the body, and childhood misunderstandings through experimental animation and moving images.

Neda Sangtarash (b. 2002, Isfahan) is an animator and experimental artist whose work explores memory, the body, and childhood misunderstandings through moving images. She began her artistic practice with storyboard, background and character design, as well as pixel art, and later studied Handicrafts, where she developed an interdisciplinary approach to animation—one grounded in experimentation with materials, form, and non-linear narration.

In her practice, Sangtarash approaches animation as a space for making the invisible tangible; a site where image replaces language, and movement becomes a tool for returning to forgotten or suppressed layers of experience.
